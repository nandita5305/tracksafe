Ten Tips For Car Accident Avoidance!

Did you know that car accidents are the leading cause of death for people aged 1-54? That’s a pretty sobering statistic, but it doesn’t have to be this way! There are plenty of things you can do to avoid getting into a car accident in the first place. No one wants to get into a car accident, but they happen all too often. In fact, according to the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration, there are more than 6 million car accidents each year in the United States. Many of these accidents could have been prevented if the drivers had taken some simple precautions. In this blog post, we will discuss some of the best ways for car accident avoidance. Stay safe on the road!

Be Aware of Your Surroundings

One of the best ways to avoid getting into an accident is to always be aware of your surroundings. You may be confident in your driving abilities, but most of the time it isn’t you, it’s someone else. Pay attention to what other drivers are doing and be prepared to react accordingly. If you see a driver who appears to be distracted or not paying attention, give them a wide berth. Watch for motorcycles or other vehicles in your blindspot. Always watch for cars changing lanes or turning without signaling.

Follow Road Laws For Car Accident Avoidance

Of course, one of the best ways to avoid getting into an accident is to simply follow the road laws. It is important to follow basic traffic laws while driving. Following these road laws will make all the difference in the world: make sure you go the speed limit, buckle your seat belt, drive sober, and don’t text and drive!

Go the Speed Limit!

Speed limits are in place for a reason and as long as you obey them, you will be less likely to get into an accident. Even if the posted speed limit is higher than what you’re comfortable driving, it’s still important to obey it as slower traffic is a cause of car accidents as well. Speeding kills, so while you may be in a hurry, it’s not worth risking your life or the lives of others by driving too fast.

Buckle Up!

Always remember to buckle up! Wearing a seatbelt is one of the simplest and most effective ways to stay safe on the road. In the unfortunate event that you do get into an accident, wearing a seatbelt could be the difference between life and death. So whether you’re driving or riding as a passenger, make sure to always buckle.

Drive Sober

Of course, one of the best ways to avoid getting into an accident is to never drink and drive. If you’re consuming alcohol, make sure to have a designated driver or take public transportation. Driving while under the influence of drugs or alcohol is not only dangerous, but it’s also illegal. If you’re taking medication, be sure to check with your doctor first to see if it’s safe to operate a vehicle. If you end up under the influence of drugs or alcohol, sleep it off, utilize many of the alternative resources available for transportation, and don’t worry about your car! Play it safe and don’t do it!

Texting and Driving

Another dangerous habit is texting and driving. It’s so easy to get distracted when you’re behind the wheel, and sending a quick text message can take your eyes off the road for just long enough to cause an accident. If you must text, pull over to a safe location first. You may think that you can safely and quickly get the message sent, but it’s not worth the risk! So make a pledge to yourself to never text and drive.

Drive Defensively

In addition to following the road laws, it’s also important to drive defensively. This means being aware of what other drivers are doing and being prepared to react accordingly. As mentioned above, if you see a driver who appears to be distracted or not paying attention, give them a wide berth. It’s also important to be aware of your surroundings and not to tailgate or cut off other drivers, and pay attention to your blindspot and other drivers’ blindspots. Watch for brake lights ahead and always give yourself and cars behind you enough time to stop. By driving defensively, you can help keep yourself and others safe on the road.

Try to Avoid Driving in Bad Weather

Bad weather can make driving conditions more treacherous and increase the chances of an accident. If you can, try to avoid driving in heavy rain or snow. If you are driving long distances, always check the weather. If you find yourself amongst a popup storm, don’t be afraid to put your hazards on and pull over. If you must drive in bad weather, be sure to take extra care and allow yourself more time to get to your destination.

Avoid Driving While Tired

If you’re feeling tired, it’s best to not drive at all for this could be the best way for car accident avoidance. Driving while tired can be just as dangerous as driving while under the influence of alcohol. Driving tired decreases awareness and your ability to drive defensively. It puts unnecessary risk on yourself and others. If you must drive tired, make sure to take breaks every few hours and find rest stops if you find yourself driving unsafe.

Report Unsafe Drivers!

If you see a driver who is behaving dangerously, be sure to report them to the authorities. There are non-emergency lines that you can call and report unsafe driving. If a driver is texting, under the influence of drugs or alcohol, tired, or they could be part of a much worse crime, by reporting it you could help prevent an accident.

Utilize Your Resources!

As you can see, there are a number of things you can do to help avoid car accidents. By following these tips, you’ll be able to stay safe on the road and help reduce your risk of being involved in an accident. Make sure to keep these things in mind the next time you get behind the wheel. Drive safe!
